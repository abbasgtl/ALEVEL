Study Plan & Strategy

Strategy

The approach to learning should align with the individual's objectives, considering factors like future profession or the preferred top university and its admission criteria. A recommended strategy is to commence with mathematics, aiming to complete the course within 8 months and subsequently taking the math exam.

Sciences

Following this, the study of chemistry can begin once a solid foundation in mathematics, particularly in ratio/proportions and percentages, is established. Biology can be pursued in the subsequent examination series. Physics is advised to be studied only after the completion of the math curriculum.

Recommendations

The optimal starting age for this academic journey is around 21 to 31 years, typically during the 7th, 8th, or 9th grade, provided the student meets the specified prerequisites. Importantly, there is no age limit for undertaking secondary examinations in the UK.

In Indian International schools, the curriculum often includes more subjects than necessary. However, it's crucial to balance this exploration with the time needed to achieve the targeted grades. Students have the flexibility to choose any subject of their preference as a private candidate through platforms like Edexcel/Cambridge.

Psychological Aspect

Students inherently possess the ability to learn quickly, particularly in subjects like Math, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. The knowledge accumulated over a 10-year period can be condensed into one or two years for slower learners, excluding social sciences.


The primary objective is to alleviate the academic burden, allowing students ample free time in their formative years to fully enjoy life and promote healthy growth, both physically and mentally.


Several factors contribute to students losing interest in studies, including an overwhelming number of subjects, insufficient explanation of concepts by teachers, and a lack of adaptation to the individual needs of students. Recognizing the unique learning styles of each student and tailoring teaching methods accordingly is essential for sustaining interest in learning.
